Red Mullet Nero Linguine

Serves: 2 adults

Cooking time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 120g of dried black linguine
  • 2 large red mullet fillets (at least 180g each)
  • ½ small fennel bulb, finely diced
  • 1 small shallot, finely diced
  • 1 red chilli, finely diced
  • 150g of organic cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbps cooking olive oil
  • 2 tbsp high quality extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ a lime, juiced
  • Sea salt
  • Black pepper
  • Chow's Double Happiness Chilli Oil

Steps

  1. Heat a wide based pan over a high heat and add in 2 tablespoons of cooking olive oil.
  2. Dry the skin on your red mullet fillets with a kitchen towel, to prevent it from sticking and generously season the flesh side with salt and pepper.
  3. Once the pan is smoking, carefully lay your red mullet fillets onto the hot pan, with the skin side down, and avoid moving them so the skin doesn’t stick to the pan.
  4. Fry the mullet fillets until 70% of the fillet is cooked, this can be seen as the flesh begins to turn white from the bottom up. Remove the fillets from the pan and reduce the pan to a medium-high heat.
  5. Add the tomatoes, fennel, shallots, garlic and chilli to the pan with a generous pinch of salt and fry them for around 10 minutes, stirring often and smashing the tomatoes.
  6. Meanwhile, boil a pan of salted water, cook the linguine according to the packaging instructions and reserve around 2 tablespoons of the water used to cook the pasta.
  7. Once the pasta is cooked, strain and add to the pan with the soft vegetables, with 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil, the saved pasta cooking water, the red mullet fillets, the juice of half the lime and stir everything well.
  8. At this point, season with salt and pepper to your taste, and finish off in the pan with our own Chow’s Double Happiness Chilli Oil.
