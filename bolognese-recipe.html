Spaghetti Bolognese

Serves: 2 adults

Cooking time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 300g high-quality ground beef
  • 1 large white onion, finely diced
  • 1 medium carrot, finely diced
  • 2 celery sticks, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 bay leaf, 2 rosemary sprigs, 2 thyme sprigs
  • 400ml hot beef stock
  • 100ml cooking red wine
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 400ml tomato passata or tinned tomatoes
  • 130g dried spaghetti
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Basil leaves to garnish
  • Parmesan to grate when serving
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chow's Double Happiness Chilli Oil

Steps

  1. Heat a wide pan up over medium to high heat, add the olive oil and the beef to the pan.
  2. Cook the beef until nicely coloured and all the moisture has been cooked out from the pan.
  3. Remove the beef from the pan, maintaining the temperature of the pan.
  4. Add to the same pan the onion, the carrot, the celery sticks, the garlic, the bay leaf, the rosemary, the thyme and 2 pinches of salt.
  5. Cook the vegetables for 7-10 minutes or until softened, stirring occasionally so as to not colour them too much.
  6. Once the vegetables have softened, add the coloured beef back into the pan, with the tomato puree and red wine.
  7. Cook it over medium heat until all the wine has evaporated.
  8. Add your hot beef stock and tomato passata to the pan and bring it up to a gentle simmer.
  9. Simmer at low heat for 1 hour or until the beef has softened and the sauce has cooked down to a thick consistency.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Boil a pan of salted water and cook the spaghetti just before serving.
  12. Serve with freshly grated parmesan, fresh basil leaves, ground black pepper, and lashings of our own Chow’s Double Happiness Chilli Oil (don’t forget to use the seeds too!).
